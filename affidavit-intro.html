Information Required for Affidavit Introductory Clause

In Ontario, Affidavit Form 4A begins with a standardized introductory clause. This section provides the court with important details about you, the deponent (the person swearing or affirming the affidavit). The information you enter here will be used to complete that introductory clause. Please provide the following details:

Your Name and Location


My Role in this Case




Ontario affidavits require you to choose either an oath or an affirmation. Swearing an oath is a religious form; affirming is a non-religious form. People choose one or the other based on personal convictions or conscience. Both choices have the same legal effect, and the court does not prefer one over the other.
